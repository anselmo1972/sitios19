Ingredients

      
  • 85 grs butter
  • 225 grs thickly cut bitter chocolate
  • 150 grs sugar
  • 2 eggs at room temperature
  • 1 generous tablespoon of bitter cocoa powder
  • 3 tablespoons cornstarch
  • optional: chopped nuts or almonds.
    •       

Process

Line a mold of 23 x 23 cm with butter paper. I was finished and I used metallic paper. Butter or spray it as the vegetable spray. Reserve. Preheat oven to 180 degrees. In another container, we sift the corn starch with the cocoa and reserve. Put the butter in a jar and melt it on a diffuser. When it is well tender we just add the chocolate, to avoid burning. Minimal fire! I put the sugar in a bowl and add the eggs one by one, beating well after each incorporation, until achieving that frothy and clear mixture that we like so much. With a wooden spatula we stir the chocolate well with the butter until achieving a tempting and velvety mixture. Then we mix it with the preparation of eggs and sugar. Finally, we take out the starch and cocoa slowly and beat with the blender vigorously for a minute. If you want, it's now time to add the nuts. Dump in the pan and take to the oven for 25/30 '. Eye, do not recook. Remove from the oven and let cool completely before cutting the brownies. Sometimes I sprinkle with impalpable sugar or with cocoa. UNMISSABLE! They can be stored at room temperature for four days, and a month in the freezer.
